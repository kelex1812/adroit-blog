Why Every Business Needs an AI Strategy in 2026

Share 𝕏 in f 🔗
Marketing June 1, 2026

2026 is the year AI moves from "interesting technology" to "table stakes." Companies that haven't built an AI strategy are already falling behind.

But here's the problem most businesses face: they know they need AI, but they don't know where to start.

The Three Waves of AI Adoption

Wave 1: Augmentation (Now)

AI that helps your team work faster:

  • AI-assisted code generation
  • AI-powered data analysis
  • AI-enhanced customer communication

ROI: Immediate productivity gains, 20-40% efficiency improvements.

Wave 2: Transformation (1-2 years)

AI that changes how you work:

  • Automated workflow orchestration
  • AI-driven decision support
  • Intelligent process automation

ROI: Structural cost reduction, 40-60% process speed improvements.

"AI adoption is a journey, not a destination. Start with the problem, not the tool."

Building Your AI Strategy: A 5-Step Framework

Step 1: Audit Your Processes

Map every business process and identify where humans spend the most time, where errors are most costly, and where decisions are repetitive.

The AI opportunity score:
Time spent (0-10) × Error cost (0-10) = Opportunity score

Step 2: Identify Quick Wins

  • Document-heavy processes
  • Data entry and reconciliation
  • Report generation
  • Customer communication triage

These are low-risk, high-impact AI opportunities.

Responsive Breakpoint Reference for Steel

Mobile (320px - 767px)

Single-column layout. Hamburger menu replaces nav. Post cards stack vertically. Category grid single column. Subscribe form stacks vertically. Reduced padding (16px). Smaller fonts: H1 at 1.5rem, cards at smaller heights.

Tablet (768px - 1023px)

Post cards: single column. Featured post: stacks vertically. Category grid: single column. Nav: full links visible. Footer: 2-column grid. Medium padding (20px).

Desktop (1024px+)

Full 2-column post grid. Featured post: 2-column with image left. Category grid: 2 columns. 4-column footer. Nav: all links visible. Max-width container: 1120px. Standard padding (24px).

Large Desktop (1280px+)

Same as desktop but with more horizontal whitespace. Container max-width remains 1120px. Centered layout. Outer padding increases to 40px.

Implementation Notes for Steel

  • Framework: Next.js App Router (already set up at ~/.hermes/kanban/workspaces/t_a20fdb1e/adroit-blog/)
  • Styling: Tailwind CSS v4 (via @import "tailwindcss" in globals.css)
  • Fonts: Google Fonts Inter — already installed in layout.tsx
  • Design tokens: Map CSS custom properties in :root to match the brand palette above
  • Breakpoints: Tailwind defaults are sm:640px, md:768px, lg:1024px, xl:1280px — align with the specs above
  • Interactivity: All hover states use transition: all 0.15s ease. Active/focus states use red ring on inputs.
  • Progress bar: Implement with a fixed 3px div at top of viewport, width updated via scroll listener
  • Category colors: Map to Tailwind semantic colors — sky, emerald, amber, pink — using the light/tint variants for backgrounds
  • Images: Post card images use CSS gradients as placeholders. Replace with actual images when available.
